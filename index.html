Purpose:

At the Ontario Native Women’s Association, there is a provincial program called “Circles of Care”. The Circles of Care Program is funded by the Ministry of Children and Youth Services through the Walking Together: Ontario’s Long-Term Strategy to End Violence Against Indigenous Women and its Family Wellbeing Program. The program is designed to meet the unique needs of Indigenous women and their children who are involved with both the child welfare system and violence against women sectors.

The program supports Indigenous women in a culturally rooted, holistic way, so that women are better able to navigate through the complex systems encountered when experiencing violence and involvement in child welfare matters. The program model supports individuals who arrive at the door “in crisis” by first stabilizing their current situation, and then building the support systems that are needed to move and stay away from crisis situations".

The Circles of Care Program focus on Indigenous women and their children who are involved in the child welfare system. This program also supports families who are experiencing or who have experienced violence in their lives. The program is designed to support families in crisis and empower women to examine their current situation and create safe and supportive means of living for themselves and their families.

VISION:

The Circles of Care Program was developed by ONWA as a result of community needs identified through:

  • Consultations with community based service providers in 9 (nine) cities across the province.
  • Numerous discussions with provincial and national groups.
  • An environmental scan of current statistics and services.
  • ONWA’s strategic planning process with members and the Board of Directors.

Goals:

The priority focus of the Circles of Care Program will be to ensure the rights of both mother and child are upheld.

Stratagies

The Circles of Care Program is designed to meet the unique needs of Indigenous women who are involved with both the child welfare system and violence against women sector.

The programme will utilize a comprehensive approach to addressing and eradicating violence against Indigenous women and their children, while enhancing supports across service areas in order to strengthen Indigenous women’s roles as leaders and caregivers in their families and communities.

Objectives

The Circles of Care Family Support Workers:
  • Advocates are needed. Women feel stronger when they have someone to help them sort through the requirements of the systems, to go to interviews and meetings with agency representatives and other service providers, and to help make sure services are working for Indigenous women.
  • It is key to develop services collaboratively, to integrate new advocacy initiatives with existing programs and to share information, training, and resources.
  • More Indigenous healing centres and community programs are needed to provide culturally restorative, holistic services for women and their families, to strengthen them and support their well-being.
  • A stronger focus on prevention and follow-up is needed, including support to women who are in crisis.
  • Education and awareness is important for Indigenous women.
  • Service providers need to show how to work with Indigenous women in a good way, through training and working side by side with Indigenous services.

    • Circles of Care Family Support Workers will focus on:
    • Crisis management and stabilization.
    • Awareness and education.
    • Planning and goal setting.
    • Liaising with other service providers.

    Outcomes

    The Circles of Care program is offered in 9 different communities across Ontario:

    1. Thunder Bay – Beendigen Inc.

    2. Sioux Lookout – Sunset Women’s Aboriginal Circle

    3. Fort Erie- Niagara Native Women Inc.

    4. Hamilton- Hamilton Wentworth Chapter of Native Women

    5. Toronto – Native Women’s Resource Centre of Toronto

    6. Midland - Georgian Bay Native Women’s Association

    7. Timmins – Ontario Native Women’s Association

    8. Ottawa- Ontario Native Women’s Association

    9. Kenora- Ontario Native Women’s Association

    Key Services of the

    Circles of Care Programmme

    CREATION OF SAFE SPACES:

    The program creates circles of care that meet cultural, physical, mental, spiritual and emotional safety needs. The Circles of Care Program creates safe spaces to share conversations, stories, and to allow women and their families to speak their truths.

    In working with women and their families the Family Support Worker will take a strengths based approach to:
    • Build trust with the woman by developing a dependable and reliable relationship with her
    • Provide crisis management and stabilization
    • Provide cultural and traditional support, through methods such as circles, teachings, ceremonies and elders
    • Inform Indigenous mothers of their rights and responsibilities
    • Inform and educate women as they navigate the complexities of the VAW or CAS systems, so that women access services they need and learn to navigate these services for themselves
    • Educate and coach the women to articulate their own concerns, needs, and goals effectively, to plan ways to move forward in their lives, and to advocate for themselves and their families
    • Provide a circle of care that responds to the cultural, mental, physical, emotional and spiritual needs of the woman through close collaboration with Indigenous and non-Indigenous services in the community

    SAFE SPACES:

    are diversely expressed and accessed at each Program site, for example: walking on nature trails, ceremony, sharing circles, culturally designed physical space and one–on-one conversations in a quiet, comfortable place. The aim of the program is to create safe spaces that are individualized to meet the Indigenous woman’s unique needs and preferences, to provide the space for her to speak her truths, access supports and tools to enhance her wellbeing, leadership, and health, without disruption.

    Circle of Care Activities

    Circle of Care activities include:

    • Working closely with individuals to provide a circle of care that responds to the cultural, mental, physical, emotional and spiritual needs of the woman through close collaboration with community services.
    • FSW’s provide services such as crisis management, traditional supports, systems navigation and advocacy support.
    • Information and education provided to mothers on their rights and responsibilities.
    • Cultural and traditional supports are an important part of programming and include sharing circles, teachings, ceremonies and guidance from elders.
    • Work with families and CAS to resolve conflicts in a way that that upholds the rights of both the mother and child.
    • Partnerships with other service providers across sectors create culturally safe systems for Indigenous women and their families to support their well-being.
